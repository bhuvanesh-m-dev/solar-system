Overview

The Solar System consists of the Sun at its centre, eight major planets, dwarf planets, countless moons, the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, the icy Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune, and the distant, spherical Oort Cloud that is the source of long‑period comets. All bodies orbit the Sun under its dominant gravity.

Belts & Clouds

Asteroid Belt

  • Location: 2.2–3.2 AU
  • Mass: ~4% of Moon
  • Notable: Ceres (dwarf planet)

Kuiper Belt

  • Location: 30–50 AU
  • Mass: ~25× Asteroid Belt
  • Notable: Pluto, Haumea, Makemake

Oort Cloud

  • Location: 2,000–100,000 AU
  • Mass: Uncertain (billions of objects)
  • Source: Long‑period comets
